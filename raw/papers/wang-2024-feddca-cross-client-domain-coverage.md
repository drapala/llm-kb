A critical consideration is how varying degrees of inter-client data heterogeneity impact the ability to construct diverse and comprehensively covering augmented datasets. Intuitively, if clients possess highly similar local data, methods relying on these local signals might struggle to achieve broad cross-client domain coverage and instruction diversity in the augmented sets, which are crucial for a well-generalized global model.\nThis section, therefore, investigates the robustness of FedDCA, Direct Retrieval, and Random Sampling to a spectrum of inter-client data heterogeneity.
