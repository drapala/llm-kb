However, investing more FLOPs per parameter makes the training process for depth-recurrent models more expensive. Our work makes significant progress towards ameliorating this issue by leveraging pretrained initializations, recurrence scheduling during training, and a data curriculum.\nHere we identify several promising avenues for future work.\nOne unsolved problem is how to most effectively build depth-recurrent models that can recur deeper at test time to solve harder problems than were seen during training.
